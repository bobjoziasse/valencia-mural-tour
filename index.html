TheWallsof El Carmen

A mural-by-mural tour of El Carmen's most significant wall paintings — what each one shows, what it means, and exactly where to find it.

FreeEntry

Start at Plaza del Tossal. The route loops through the core of El Carmen, ending near the Torres de Serranos. Walk in order, or cherry-pick by mural — each card tells you what to look for on the wall, not just which street to find it on.

⚠ Street art is ephemeral. Major works documented here have stood for years, but murals can change overnight. Expect the unexpected — and check every corner you weren't planning to.
01
Plaza del Tossal · Start here · Right wall (east side)
Divine Moses
Blu · Italian · 2011
Plaza del Tossal, El Carmen — right wall facing the square
🐍
Moses · Blu · Plaza del Tossal · 2011
Right (east) wall of Plaza del Tossal
Painted 2011 · One of the most photographed walls in Spain
▶ What you are looking at

A full building-height robed figure of Moses. His beard does not end in hair — it dissolves into a writhing mass of snakes. In each hand he holds a stone tablet. Instead of the Ten Commandments, the tablets bear a dollar sign and a euro sign.

Blu is one of the world's most significant muralists. This is his most famous work in Spain. Moses — the lawgiver — is recast as a servant of money. The snakes replace divine wisdom with venomous authority. It was painted during the European debt crisis of 2011. That context is inseparable from the image.

📸Cross to the café terrace on the opposite side of the square — the only angle where both this mural and Escif's falling cars fit in a single frame. Morning light hits this wall from the east before noon.
→
Stay in Plaza del Tossal. The next mural is on the adjacent wall — turn left, same square.
10m
02
Plaza del Tossal · Left wall (west side)
Falling Cars
Escif · Valencian · 2011
Plaza del Tossal, El Carmen — left wall facing the square
🚗
Falling Cars · Escif · 2011
Left (west) wall of Plaza del Tossal — same square as Moses
Painted 2011 · Still standing
▶ What you are looking at

Multiple ordinary cars cascade down the building facade as if they drove off the roof. The palette is near-monochrome — greys, off-whites, muted ochre. The cars look perfectly normal. They are just falling. There is no crash. No drama. Just the fall, frozen.

Escif is Valencia's most celebrated urban artist — anonymous by choice. His signature is deadpan surrealism: recognisable objects removed from logic. Consumer aspirations — cars, status, progress — simply falling, pointless, defying sense. The stillness is what unsettles. He never explains his work. He leaves that entirely to you.

👁Stand well back from the square before approaching. These are 4–5 storey walls. You need distance to read them as full compositions.
→
Walk east along Carrer de Cavallers past Plaza de Sant Jaume. Turn right (south) into Carrer Baja.

03
Carrer Baja · Corner building · Two faces
Rabbit & Chicken
Ericailcane · Italian
Carrer Baix (Calle Baja), El Carmen — corner building, walk both sides
🐰
Rabbit & Chicken · Ericailcane · Calle Baja
Two opposite faces of the same corner building on Carrer Baja
Carrer Baja · El Carmen · Documented
▶ What you are looking at

A giant rabbit has its hands around the throat of a large chicken. Below them: a paella pan. In the background, a crowd of grey, faceless people watches without moving. The two animals are on opposite sides of the same corner building — walk around to see both.

Ericailcane (Bologna, Italy) is known internationally for anthropomorphic animals in confrontation. The subject here is painfully local: traditional paella valenciana uses rabbit and chicken — not seafood. The mural stages a gladiatorial fight to decide which ingredient earns its place in the dish. The grey crowd watching passively amplifies the absurdity. Deadpan comedy about a matter of genuine local seriousness.

🔄You must walk around the corner to see the companion piece on the other face. The two sides form one complete narrative — don't miss it.
→
Continue along Carrer Baja toward number 29. Look right at the wall.

04
Carrer Baja, No. 29
Portrait
Xemayo · Valencian
Carrer Baja No. 29, El Carmen — right-hand wall
🎭
Portrait · Xemayo · Calle Baja 29
Wall at Carrer Baja No. 29
Carrer Baja No. 29 · Documented
▶ What you are looking at

A large-format figurative portrait. Xemayo uses strong outlines and concentrated detail on the face, with flatter handling elsewhere. The subject is anonymous — no name, no caption. The face commands the wall without explaining itself.

Xemayo is a Valencian street portraitist. In a neighbourhood saturated with political murals and surreal commentary, a portrait that simply is — no message, no symbol — stands as its own kind of refusal. The face asks only to be looked at.

🔍Calle Baja is one of the richest single streets in El Carmen. Walk the full length slowly, both sides, checking every wall section, gate, and door.

05
Carrer Moro Zaid · Facing the old car park
Girl with Cats & Flowers
Julieta XLF · Spanish · XLF Crew
Carrer Moro Zaid, El Carmen — wall facing the old car park
🌸
Girl with Cats · Julieta XLF · Calle Moro Zaid
Carrer Moro Zaid, facing the old car park
El Carmen · Julieta XLF · Documented
▶ What you are looking at

A tall, elongated female figure surrounded by cats and botanical forms — flowers, leaves, organic shapes in muted dusty pinks, sage greens, and cream. The cats press close to the figure. Everything is in Julieta's characteristic fluid line: soft but precise.

Julieta XLF is part of the XLF collective and Valencia's most prolific female muralist. Her figures are almost always women — in solitude, in relationship with nature or animals rather than other people. There is a stillness in her work that persists against the noise of the city around it. She has documented murals across Calle Baja, Calle de la Beneficència, Calle Ripalda, Calle Doctor Chiarri, and Calle Roteros.

📍Once you recognise her style — those elongated proportions, soft palettes, botanical backgrounds — you will spot her work on multiple other walls as you continue the route.
→
Walk west along Carrer de la Beneficència toward the back of the IVAM museum. The mural runs the full length of the park wall behind the building.
350m
06
Behind IVAM Modern Art Museum · Carrer de la Beneficència
IVAM Wall
Escif · Valencian · Institutional commission
Rear wall of IVAM, Carrer de Guillem de Castro / Carrer de la Beneficència
🏛️
IVAM Wall · Escif · Institutional Commission
The entire rear facade of the IVAM — Valencia's foremost modern art museum — painted by Escif
IVAM commission · Escif · Valencia's largest institutional mural
▶ What you are looking at

The entire back wall of the IVAM painted as a single composition. Classical Roman busts sit alongside what look like ordinary graffiti tags from the neighbourhood. One section shows a white unicorn with the hand-painted phrase "Fuera droga del barrio" (Get drugs out of the neighbourhood), painted in 2017. The scale requires distance — stand back in the park to read it fully.

The IVAM commissioned Escif — a remarkable institutional act. He responded by painting the museum with the actual language of the streets around it: the graffiti tags visible in the mural are real messages Escif collected from Carmen's walls and transposed onto the museum's surface. Classical art and street scrawl share the same canvas. The museum becomes the street. The street becomes the museum. It's his most complex and layered single work in Valencia.

🪑Sit on the park benches opposite. Read left to right. The unicorn and anti-drug inscription are on the right section. Roman busts are centre-left. Give it 10 minutes — it keeps revealing itself.
→

07
Block near Plaza Mossén Sorell · El Carmen
Mummified Spaceman
Deih · Valencian · XLF Crew
Wall on the block around Plaza Mossén Sorell · El Carmen
👾
Mummified Spaceman · Deih · Near Plaza Mossén Sorell
Wall near Plaza Mossén Sorell — Julieta XLF has a companion piece on the same block
XLF Crew · El Carmen · Near Plaza Mossén Sorell
▶ What you are looking at

A large figure that reads as simultaneously mummified and space-suited — wrapped in bandages or a suit, the body is deliberately ambiguous between ancient and futuristic. Geometric forms, flat colours, bold comic-book outlines. The figure occupies the wall with authority but no aggression.

Deih (XLF crew) builds a visual language that merges sci-fi, hip-hop, and medieval iconography. His figures are alien to their environment — literally alien, but also socially displaced. He grew up painting Valencia and remains one of its most internationally exhibited urban artists. A Julieta XLF piece is documented on the same block — the two paint in close proximity as part of the same crew.

👀Also look at Plaza Mossén Sorell's children's playground — surfaces painted with cartoon characters. It documents how murals permeate every surface in this barrio, not just the prestige walls.

08
Plaza del Carmen · CIMA Building — three painted sides
Valencia Panorama
Cabiscol · Valencian local painter
CIMA building, corner of Calle Moret and Plaza del Carmen — walk all three sides
🏙️
Valencia Panorama · Cabiscol · Plaza del Carmen
All three exterior walls of the CIMA building wrap around with panoramic painted Valencia scenes
Plaza del Carmen · Three-sided building · Cabiscol
▶ What you are looking at

The headquarters of CIMA — an association for older Valencian residents — has all three exterior walls painted by local artist Cabiscol. The murals are panoramic: Valencia's towers, bridges, countryside, animals, and city life wrap around the building like a painted postcard. Each side is a chapter of the same landscape.

Cabiscol brings a different register to El Carmen — less political, more celebratory. The subject is Valencia as its residents see it and love it. This is a building for older citizens, painted with the visual identity of their city. It sits in contrast to the confrontational works nearby, and is more affecting for that difference.

🔄Walk the complete perimeter of the building before moving on — the three sides read as one continuous composition. The corner where Calle Moret meets the plaza is where it begins.

09
Carrer de Moret — "Calle de los Colores"
The Street of Colours
Multiple artists · #KissmeVLC kissing couple
Carrer de Moret (Calle Moret), El Carmen — full length of the pedestrian street
💑
Street of Colours · Multiple artists · Calle Moret
Carrer de Moret — every wall, shutter, drainpipe, and door is painted by different hands
Ongoing · Multiple years · Nicknamed Calle de los Colores
▶ What you are looking at

An entire short pedestrian lane where every surface — walls, shutters, window surrounds, drainpipes, utility boxes — has been painted by different artists at different times. The signature centrepiece is a large mural of a kissing couple. The image was taken from a photo captured at London Bridge Station on 14 August 2012. Using the hashtag #kissmevlc, the city tried to identify the couple. They were never found. They are still on the wall.

This street is the cumulative effect rather than any single named work. But the kissing couple is its anchor: a real anonymous moment of intimacy, captured by a stranger, painted onto a wall in another country, claimed as a Valencian act. The neighbourhood adopted them without knowing who they were.

🕗Morning is best here — the street is narrow and shaded. When shops are closed, the painted shutters add more surface than when they are open. Come back at night to see how the light changes everything.
→

10
Carrer Roteros + all over El Carmen
The Masked Figure
David de Limón · Valencian
Carrer Roteros — and everywhere in El Carmen, look high and low
🥷
The Masked Figure · David de Limón · All of El Carmen
David de Limón's figures appear at all scales — hand-sized in corners, multi-storey on building faces
Ongoing · Ubiquitous across all El Carmen
▶ What you are looking at

A black-and-white masked figure — often described by viewers as a ninja or a karate fighter. Always carrying a red heart-shaped symbol on its chest. Always monochrome. It appears at wildly different scales: tiny, tucked above a doorway; or multiple metres tall on a full building face. Once you spot one, you see them everywhere.

David de Limón is Valencia's most pervasive street presence. In an interview, he clarified: it is not a ninja — it is a masked character dedicated to painting the city. The mask is anonymity. The heart is affection. The ubiquity is the entire point — the figure haunts El Carmen like a benevolent ghost, appearing on bins, above exits, on private doors, in narrow alleys nobody photographed before.

🎮Count them across the full route — they appear from Stop 1 onward. Average across the full walk: 8 to 12 sightings. The smallest ones reward the most attention.

The Artists

The six artists whose named works anchor this tour — all either Valencian-born or adopted the city as home.

Blu
Italian · International

One of the world's most significant muralists. His Moses at Plaza del Tossal (2011) is his most famous work in Spain. Consistently political: money, power, and institutional religion are his targets. Rarely grants interviews. Works globally.

Escif
Valencia, Spain · Anonymous

The most celebrated urban artist to come out of Valencia. Anonymous. Uses flat colour and minimal line to deliver deadpan social commentary. Commissioned to paint the entire rear wall of the IVAM — one of the most significant institutional recognitions of street art in Spain.

Hyuro
Argentina, based Valencia · 1974–2020

Tamara Djurovic. Moved from Argentina to Valencia for her studies and stayed. Monochromatic figurative work: faceless women in oversized dimensions addressing gender violence and city life. Passed away in 2020. Her murals remain across El Carmen.

Julieta XLF
Spain · XLF Collective

Most prolific female muralist in Valencia. Elongated female figures in muted botanical palettes. Part of the XLF crew with Deih. Has documented works on Calle Moro Zaid, Calle Baja, Calle de la Beneficència, Calle Ripalda, Calle Doctor Chiarri, and Calle Roteros.

Deih
Valencia, Spain · XLF Collective

Comic-book influenced forms fusing sci-fi, hip-hop, and medieval iconography. Internationally exhibited. Often paints in close proximity with Julieta XLF. His figures are deliberately alien to their surroundings — displaced presences on familiar walls.

David de Limón
Valencia, Spain

Creator of the masked, red-hearted character that appears across every surface in El Carmen. Described the figure himself as a masked person "dedicated to painting" the city — not a ninja. The red heart is always present. Finding all the hidden figures is its own reward.

Practical

Start Point
Plaza del Tossal. Metro: Àngel Guimerà (L1/L5) + 8 min walk north.
Best Light
08:00–10:00 for empty streets and low angle. 17:00–19:00 for warm tones on plaster.
Duration
1.5 hrs walking. Allow 2.5 hrs if shooting seriously at every stop.
Cost
Free. No admission. No reservation. The street is the gallery.
Key Rule
Always check around every corner. The best pieces in El Carmen are never on the obvious wall.
Murals Change
Major works (Blu, Escif) have stood for years. Smaller pieces come and go overnight. Expect change.
