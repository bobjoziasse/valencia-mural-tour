01
Plaza del Tossal · Start here · Right wall (east side)
Divine Moses
Blu · Italian · 2011
Plaza del Tossal, El Carmen — right wall facing the square Directions
🐍
Moses · Blu · Plaza del Tossal · 2011
Right (east) wall of Plaza del Tossal
Painted 2011 · One of the most photographed walls in Spain
▶ What you are looking at

A full building-height robed figure of Moses. His beard does not end in hair — it dissolves into a writhing mass of snakes. In each hand he holds a stone tablet. Instead of the Ten Commandments, the tablets bear a dollar sign and a euro sign.

Blu is one of the world's most significant muralists. This is his most famous work in Spain. Moses — the lawgiver — is recast as a servant of money. The snakes replace divine wisdom with venomous authority. Painted during the European debt crisis of 2011. That context is inseparable from the image.

📸Cross to the café terrace on the opposite side of the square — the only angle where both this mural and Escif's falling cars fit in one frame. Morning light hits this wall from the east before noon.
→
Stay in Plaza del Tossal. Turn left — the next mural is on the adjacent wall, same square.

02
Plaza del Tossal · Left wall (west side)
Falling Cars
Escif · Valencian · 2011
Plaza del Tossal, El Carmen — left wall facing the square Directions
🚗
Falling Cars · Escif · 2011
Left (west) wall of Plaza del Tossal — same square as Moses
Painted 2011 · Still standing
▶ What you are looking at

Multiple ordinary cars cascade down the building facade as if they drove off the roof. Near-monochrome palette — greys, off-whites, muted ochre. The cars look completely normal. They are just falling. No crash. No drama. Just the fall, frozen.

Escif is Valencia's most celebrated urban artist — anonymous by choice. His signature is deadpan surrealism: recognisable objects removed from logic. Consumer aspirations — cars, status, progress — simply falling, pointless. The stillness is what unsettles. He never explains his work.

👁Stand well back from the square before approaching — these are 4–5 storey walls. Distance is required to read them as full compositions.
→

03
Carrer Baja · Corner building · Two faces
Rabbit & Chicken
Ericailcane · Italian
Carrer Baix (Calle Baja), El Carmen — corner building Directions
🐰
Rabbit & Chicken · Ericailcane · Calle Baja
Two opposite faces of the same corner building on Carrer Baja
Carrer Baja · El Carmen
▶ What you are looking at

A giant rabbit has its hands around the throat of a large chicken. Below them: a paella pan. In the background, a crowd of grey, faceless people watches without moving. The animals are on opposite sides of the same corner building — walk around to see both faces.

Ericailcane (Bologna) is known for anthropomorphic animals in confrontation. The subject is painfully local: traditional paella valenciana uses rabbit and chicken — not seafood. The mural stages a gladiatorial fight to decide which ingredient earns its place in the dish. Deadpan comedy about a matter of genuine local seriousness.

🔄You must walk around the corner to see the companion piece on the other face. The two sides form one complete narrative.
→
Continue along Carrer Baja toward number 29. Look right at the wall.

Carrer Baja, No. 29
Portrait
Xemayo · Valencian
Carrer Baja No. 29, El Carmen — right-hand wall Directions
🎭
Portrait · Xemayo · Calle Baja 29
Wall at Carrer Baja No. 29
Carrer Baja No. 29 · Documented
▶ What you are looking at

A large-format figurative portrait. Strong outlines and concentrated detail on the face, with flatter handling elsewhere. The subject is anonymous — no name, no caption. The face commands the wall without explaining itself.

Xemayo is a Valencian street portraitist. In a neighbourhood saturated with political murals and surreal commentary, a portrait that simply is stands as its own refusal. The face asks only to be looked at.

🔍Calle Baja is one of the richest single streets in El Carmen — walk the full length slowly, both sides, checking every wall section, gate, and door.

05
Carrer Moro Zaid · Facing the old car park
Girl with Cats & Flowers
Julieta XLF · Spanish · XLF Crew
Carrer Moro Zaid, El Carmen — wall facing old car park Directions
🌸
Girl with Cats · Julieta XLF · Calle Moro Zaid
Carrer Moro Zaid, facing the old car park
El Carmen · Julieta XLF
▶ What you are looking at

A tall, elongated female figure surrounded by cats and botanical forms — flowers, leaves, organic shapes in muted dusty pinks, sage greens, and cream. The cats press close. Everything in Julieta's characteristic fluid line: soft but precise.

Julieta XLF is Valencia's most prolific female muralist. Her figures are almost always women — in solitude, in relationship with nature or animals. There is a stillness in her work that persists against the noise of the city around it. She has documented murals across Calle Moro Zaid, Calle Baja, Calle de la Beneficència, Calle Ripalda, and Calle Roteros.

📍Once you recognise her style — elongated proportions, soft palettes, botanical backgrounds — you will spot her work on multiple other walls through the rest of the route.
→
Walk west along Carrer de la Beneficència toward the back of the IVAM museum. The mural runs the full park wall behind the building.

06
Behind IVAM Museum · Carrer de la Beneficència
IVAM Wall
Escif · Valencian · Institutional commission
Rear wall of IVAM, Carrer de Guillem de Castro Directions
🏛️
IVAM Wall · Escif · Institutional Commission
The entire rear facade of the IVAM modern art museum, painted by Escif
IVAM commission · Valencia's largest institutional mural
▶ What you are looking at

The entire back wall of the IVAM. Classical Roman busts sit alongside what look like ordinary neighbourhood graffiti tags. One section: a white unicorn with the text "Fuera droga del barrio" (Get drugs out of the neighbourhood), 2017. Stand back in the park to read it all — the wall is enormous.

The IVAM commissioned Escif — a remarkable institutional act. He responded by painting the museum with real messages collected from Carmen's own walls, transposed onto an institutional surface. Classical art and street scrawl share the same canvas. The museum becomes the street. The street becomes the museum.

🪑Sit on the park benches opposite and read left to right slowly. The unicorn and anti-drug inscription are on the right section. Give it 10 minutes — it keeps revealing itself.
→

👾
Mummified Spaceman · Deih · Near Plaza Mossén Sorell
Block near Plaza Mossén Sorell — Julieta XLF has a companion piece on the same block
XLF Crew · El Carmen
▶ What you are looking at

A large figure that reads as simultaneously mummified and space-suited — wrapped, ambiguous between ancient and futuristic. Geometric forms, flat colours, bold comic-book outlines. The figure occupies the wall with authority but no aggression.

Deih (XLF crew) builds a visual language merging sci-fi, hip-hop, and medieval iconography. His figures are deliberately alien to their surroundings — displaced presences on familiar walls. A Julieta XLF piece is on the same block — they frequently paint in close proximity.

👀Also check the children's playground at Plaza Mossén Sorell — cartoon characters painted on all surfaces. Documents how murals permeate every level in this neighbourhood, not just the prestige walls.

Plaza del Carmen · CIMA Building — three painted sides
Valencia Panorama
Cabiscol · Valencian local painter
CIMA building, corner of Calle Moret and Plaza del Carmen Directions
🏙️
Valencia Panorama · Cabiscol · Plaza del Carmen
All three exterior walls of the CIMA building wrap in panoramic painted Valencia scenes
Plaza del Carmen · Three-sided building
▶ What you are looking at

All three exterior walls of the CIMA building are painted by local artist Cabiscol. The murals are panoramic: Valencia's towers, bridges, countryside, animals, and city life wrap around the building. Each side is a chapter of the same landscape.

Cabiscol brings a different register to El Carmen — less political, more celebratory and civic. This is a building for older citizens, painted with their city's identity. It sits in contrast to the confrontational works nearby, and is more affecting for that difference.

🔄Walk the full perimeter of the building — the three sides read as one continuous composition. Begin at the corner where Calle Moret meets the plaza.

09
Carrer de Moret — "Calle de los Colores"
The Street of Colours
Multiple artists · #KissmeVLC couple
Carrer de Moret (Calle Moret), El Carmen — full street Directions
💑
Street of Colours · Multiple artists · Calle Moret
Carrer de Moret — every wall, shutter, drainpipe, and door is painted by different hands
Ongoing · Multiple years · Nicknamed Calle de los Colores
▶ What you are looking at

An entire pedestrian lane where every surface — walls, shutters, window surrounds, drainpipes, utility boxes — has been painted by different artists. The centrepiece: a large mural of a kissing couple, taken from a photo at London Bridge Station on 14 August 2012. The hashtag #kissmevlc tried to identify them. They were never found. They are still on the wall.

Less about any single named work, more about total saturation. The kissing couple is its anchor: a real anonymous moment of intimacy, captured by a stranger, painted onto a wall in another country, claimed as a Valencian act.

🕗Morning is best — the lane is narrow and shaded. When shops are closed, painted shutters reveal more surface than when they are open. Come back at night to see it completely transformed.
→
Head north on Carrer Roteros toward Torres de Serranos. Final mural-dense street of the route.

10
Carrer Roteros + all over El Carmen
The Masked Figure
David de Limón · Valencian
Carrer Roteros — and everywhere in El Carmen, look high and low Directions
🥷
The Masked Figure · David de Limón · All of El Carmen
David de Limón's figures appear at all scales — hand-sized in corners, multi-storey on building faces
Ongoing · Ubiquitous across all El Carmen
▶ What you are looking at

A black-and-white masked figure — often described as a ninja, though the artist says it is simply a masked character. Always carrying a red heart-shaped symbol on its chest. Always monochrome. Appears at wildly different scales from tiny corner pieces to multi-storey walls. Once you spot one, you see them everywhere.

David de Limón is Valencia's most pervasive street presence. The mask is anonymity. The heart is affection. The ubiquity is the entire point — the figure haunts El Carmen like a benevolent ghost, appearing on bins, above exits, on private doors, in alleys nobody else photographed.

🎮Count them across the full route — they appear from Stop 1 onward. Average across the full walk: 8 to 12 sightings. The smallest ones reward the most attention.

The Artists

Six artists whose named works anchor this tour — all either Valencian-born or adopted the city as home.

Blu
Italian · International

One of the world's most significant muralists. His Moses at Plaza del Tossal (2011) is his most famous work in Spain. Consistently political: money, power, and institutional religion are his targets.

Escif
Valencia, Spain · Anonymous

The most celebrated urban artist to come out of Valencia. Anonymous. Uses flat colour and minimal line for deadpan social commentary. Commissioned to paint the entire rear wall of the IVAM — one of the most significant institutional recognitions of street art in Spain.

Hyuro
Argentina, based Valencia · 1974–2020

Tamara Djurovic. Monochromatic figurative work: faceless women in outsized dimensions addressing gender violence and city life. Passed away in 2020. Her murals remain across El Carmen.

Julieta XLF
Spain · XLF Collective

Most prolific female muralist in Valencia. Elongated female figures in muted botanical palettes. Part of the XLF crew with Deih. Works documented on Calle Moro Zaid, Calle Baja, Calle de la Beneficència, Calle Ripalda, and Calle Roteros.

Deih
Valencia, Spain · XLF Collective

Comic-book forms fusing sci-fi, hip-hop, and medieval iconography. Internationally exhibited. Frequently paints in proximity with Julieta XLF. His figures are deliberately alien to their surroundings.

David de Limón
Valencia, Spain

Creator of the masked, red-hearted character that haunts every corner of El Carmen. Described the figure himself as a masked person "dedicated to painting" — not a ninja. The red heart is always present.

Practical

Start Point
Plaza del Tossal. Metro: Àngel Guimerà (L1/L5) + 8 min walk north.
Best Light
08:00–10:00 for empty streets + low angle. 17:00–19:00 for warm tones on plaster.
Duration
1.5 hrs walking. Allow 2.5 hrs if shooting seriously at every stop.
Cost
Free. No admission. No reservation. The street is the gallery.
Key Rule
Always check around every corner. The best pieces in El Carmen are never on the obvious wall.
Murals Change
Major works (Blu, Escif) have stood for years. Smaller pieces come and go overnight.
